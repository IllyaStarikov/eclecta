### [Ask Twice, Look Twice: Prompt Echoing Resolves the Question-First Paradox in Vision-Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15565) — 6/10

A prompt-only edit, restating the question before and after the image, recovers up to 19 Winoground group-accuracy points on open VLMs with no training, fine-tuning, or architecture change.

Notes: The claimed 'question-first paradox': putting the question before the image underperforms the image-first ordering recommended for frontier VLMs across VQA benchmarks, at a cost of up to 17.5 group-accuracy points across five open VLMs. · Diagnosis via logit-lens and attention probes: a question placed before the image does steer perception (image patch representations move toward question-relevant concepts), but stranded behind hundreds of image tokens it is barely attended by the answer token, which commits to image-driven, often wrong answers. · A causal attention knockout is offered as evidence that the answer token reads the question only when the question follows the image, isolating a downstream readout failure from the upstream perception effect. · The fix, 'question echoing': restate the question on both sides of the image so one copy steers perception and the other is read out at answer time; echoing the image as well is claimed to restore the whole-image view a causal decoder otherwise loses. · Reported results: echoed prompts surpass the best single-pass ordering on NaturalBench, POPE, Winoground, and open-ended VQAv2, by up to 19 Winoground group-accuracy points, with no training or architecture change. · Framed as a parallel to a fifty-year-old human 'adjunct questions' finding: repeating a question before and after a passage aids comprehension more than either position alone; all numbers are self-reported and limited to five open-weight VLMs.

### [Slater – Low-memory graphdb designed for read-heavy graphs](https://github.com/Hikari-Systems/slater) — 5/10

If your graph is bigger than your RAM budget, Slater claims to serve a 90M-node/1.5B-edge graph from a few hundred MB by paging from disk, so you can run stateless Neo4j-compatible read replicas without provisioning 64-128 GiB per instance.

Notes: Decouples RAM from graph size: you set a fixed cache budget across three bounded pools (decompressed-block LRU, vector-index pool, result LRU), and the graph is paged from an on-disk image via positional reads (never mmap). Self-reported: the Wikidata 90M-node/1.5B-edge graph serves from a few hundred MB where in-memory engines need ~64-128 GiB resident. · Two binaries: slater-build compiles a Cypher dump offline into an immutable, BLAKE3-content-hashed generation directory (zstd blocks, ISAM range indexes, Vamana+PQ vector indexes); slater serves it over Bolt 5.4/4.4/4.1 so standard neo4j drivers work unchanged. Deployment is a file swap: build a new generation, atomically flip the current pointer, torn/half-copied images are refused via re-hash at open. · Opt-in writable layer (delta.enabled) is a single-writer LSM over the immutable core: WAL + memtable spilling to L0 delta segments, folded back by consolidation (manual CALL slater.consolidate(), size-triggered, or windowed). Acknowledged means fsync-durable; a write-UNWIND commits one fsync per batch. No multi-statement transactions and no rollback; writes are business-key MERGE/SET/DELETE addressed by identity property. · Read path is byte-identical when the delta is empty (monomorphised ReadView, single predictable branch). Whole-graph counters like count(*) and label/reltype marginals come from the delta's own live counters: claim is tens of ms over a 91.6M-node core with ~500k pending writes without touching a block. Read cost of a write scales with delta size, not graph size. · Disk-native ANN vector search (Vamana graph + product-quantised codes kept resident; cosine/L2/dot) sits next to the graph as a RAG retrieval layer; brute-force below the --ann-threshold (default 50,000 vectors). Embeddings are writable in place via a FreshDiskANN-style ladder (base + per-segment + in-memory RW index), KNN-visible immediately, no offline rebuild. · Storage backends: local fs (default, full BLAKE3 re-hash at open), S3/S3-compatible, and GCS, selectable by config, same byte format across all. WAL is local-disk only (delta.walDir), so a writer node is stateful while read replicas stay stateless. Extras: XChaCha20-Poly1305 at-rest sealing, TLS Bolt, argon2id ACLs, ~22 MB distroless image, #![forbid(unsafe_code)] outside the jemalloc crate. All performance figures are the author's own; no independent benchmarks provided.
