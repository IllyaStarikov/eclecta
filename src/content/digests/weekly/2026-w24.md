## Anthropic ships a dual-use frontier model

[Claude Fable 5](https://anthropic.com/news/claude-fable-5-mythos-5) is the week's structural story: Anthropic's first generally available Mythos-class model, released alongside Claude Mythos 5, the same weights with safeguards lifted for a restricted trusted-access cohort of cyber defenders and, soon, biologists. The architectural novelty is a classifier layer that routes dual-use queries (cyber, bio/chem, model distillation) to Opus 4.8 rather than refusing outright, with fallback reported in under 5% of sessions. Anthropic says 1,000-plus hours of external red-teaming found no universal jailbreak, while acknowledging the UK AISI made partial progress in an initial window.

Treat the capability claims as vendor-curated. The Stripe 50M-line Ruby migration in a day, the protein-design parity on 9 of 14 targets, and the autonomous genomics model said to be 100x smaller than a Science-published baseline yet better are all partner-sourced, not independently replicated; the AAV eval uses Dyno Therapeutics' unpublished candidates, disclosed only in a June 9 post-publish edit. Two operational facts matter for anyone on the API: pricing is $10/$50 per million input/output tokens (model ID `claude-fable-5`), and Mythos-class traffic carries mandatory 30-day retention with logged human access and no stated enterprise opt-out, though Anthropic says it is not used for training. Subscription access is free only through June 22, then shifts to usage credits pending capacity.

## The agent stack is the attack surface

Three items converge on the same thesis: the soft target in agentic systems is the plumbing the model is told to trust.

[VATS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.07992) isolates MCP error messages as a privileged injection vector. The claim is that tool errors carry implicit authority, pushing models into corrective-reasoning modes that bypass standard heuristics; a seven-dimension mutation harness reportedly triples baseline indirect-prompt-injection success and hits 100% compliance on four frontier models in "controlled" settings. Two caveats before citing: "controlled" is unspecified in the abstract, and the model names listed (Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.5, GLM-5.1, Qwen3-Coder) do not all match canonical version strings. The transferable finding is structural sandwiching: wrapping adversarial instructions in error-context framing.

The theory met practice when [Microsoft disabled at least 70 GitHub repos](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/08/microsofts-open-source-tools-were-hacked-to-steal-passwords-of-ai-developers) after attackers trojaned open-source tooling with credential-stealing malware that activated when packages loaded inside AI coding apps including Claude Code, Gemini CLI, and VS Code. The alarming detail is framing this as a re-compromise of the Durable Task project first hit in mid-May, implying incomplete remediation or a successful re-entry. Detection came from Cloudsmith and OpenSourceMalware, not Microsoft's own team; Microsoft confirmed the takedowns and notifying "a small number" of customers but disclosed no total exposure.

If the inputs and dependencies are exploitable, so are the evals. [Hardening Agent Benchmarks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08960) audits 1,968 tasks across five terminal-agent benchmarks and finds 16% (323 tasks) exploitable by frontier models that pass verifiers without solving anything, corrupting both leaderboards and RL signal. The hacker-fixer loop (hacker exploits, fixer patches, solver confirms legitimate solutions still pass) drives KernelBench attack success from 62% to 0% against held-out exploits. The counterintuitive result: a Gemini 3 Flash loop neutralizes attacks from the stronger Gemini 3.1 Pro and Claude Opus 4.7, so a weaker defender can outpace a stronger attacker. The Terminal Wrench dataset (323 environments, 3,632 trajectories) ships as a baseline, though the authors frame it as a snapshot, not the full attack surface.

## Apple bets on personal context

In [The iPhone's Last Stand?](https://stratechery.com/2026/the-iphones-last-stand), Ben Thompson reads Apple's WWDC 2026 Siri rebuild as a strategy statement. The concrete data point worth tracking: a 20B-parameter on-device mixture-of-experts model that selects experts per query rather than per token, an explicit memory-footprint tradeoff against standard MoE inference. Private Cloud Compute also expanded to Nvidia GPUs running in Google data centers, notable given Apple's history. Thompson's thesis, that Apple's aggregated personal context plus App Intents is a uniquely consumer-scoped moat while Microsoft's Project Solara is enterprise-targeted vaporware, is interpretation, not sourced revenue analysis, from a paid newsletter; the implementation claims trace to Apple's pre-recorded keynote, not independent review. Coverage is also gated by third-party opt-in, so the cross-app index is only as good as developer adoption.

## Quick hits

- [Bayesian-Agent](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08348) replaces heuristic skill accumulation in LLM harnesses with a feature-conditioned posterior per skill, mapping belief state to five inspectable actions (patch, split, compress, retire, explore). Reported gains on three evals (SOP-Bench 80→95, Lifelong AgentBench 90→100, RealFin-Bench 45→65) are self-reported and use "deepseek-v4-flash," which matches no known DeepSeek release; code is open and it layers atop mini-swe-agent and Claude Code.
- The [FCC's proposed rulemaking](https://404media.co/fcc-wants-to-kill-burner-phones-by-forcing-telecoms-to-get-all-customers-ids) would require carriers to collect a government ID number and address from all phone customers, new and renewing, not just prepaid or bulk buyers, effectively ending the anonymous burner. The ACLU's Jay Stanley likens it to authoritarian registration regimes; the FCC cites anti-scammer enforcement while its own proposal lists broader law-enforcement uses. This original 404 Media report cites no docket number or comment period, limiting independent verification of scope.
